Start · Stop · Keep

What the team is telling us — counted, not vibes

A theme appears below only if 4 or more people raised it independently (≈8%+ of 52 respondents). Counts are shown. Strong one-off ideas live in the Signals box — as signals, not trends.

Start

Let's get moving on…

Manager development & the bench8 of 52

Structured training for new managers, SOPs per role, mentorship, BM pipeline. The dominant ask across both regions and CET — the field independently voted for the Q3 "speed to competence" direction.

"Build more structured leadership training and mentorship for new and existing managers." — Frank Williams, Leander
Career-path clarity & reviews5 of 52

Clear benchmarks from Lead → management, lead pay differentiation, annual reviews for non-commissioned staff (incl. PH team post-Rippling).

Cross-team connection5 of 52

Mover-manager + upper-management roundtables, manager outings, family events, paid volunteer hours.

Flexibility for management4 of 52

Extend flex days to leadership/management; 4-day-week and WFH-day experiments. Raised at every level, including Roundtable.

Stop

Pump the brakes on…

Pace of change / initiative churn6 of 52

The loudest STOP. Initiatives shift before they land; policies change mid-quarter (driver scoring changed 3× in one quarter); rollouts outpace the team's capacity to absorb.

"Be intentional about the pace of change… the majority of regular people do not have the capacity to absorb all of the changes meaningfully." — Anne Bosse
Sales blocks & goals for BMs4 of 52

Four branch leaders independently: BM sales-block hours cost the branch more than they earn, especially in hiring-heavy months. Feeds directly into the manager sales-bandwidth review already scheduled.

Unverified AI & impersonal touchpoints4 of 52

Not anti-AI — anti-unverified AI: agent replies to branch emails felt impersonal; AI-produced data that can't be quickly checked creates stress. Counter-current: 4 others named AI leverage a top KEEP. The ask is verification norms + human moments.

The good stuff

Recognition & celebration culture15 of 52

Praise, Legends, Magic Moments, parties, conference, quarterly prizes — the overwhelming KEEP. One refinement from within the love: keep it authentic; quota-driven praise can start to feel forced.

"This level of psychological safety and accessible leadership is incredibly rare." — Arden Asilo, CET
Bonuses & incentives6 of 52

Margin bonus, quarterly prizes, monetary incentives — and "keep paying off this new office. I love it!"

Open-door leadership6 of 52

Access to leadership and open communication about direction, named repeatedly as what makes Einstein different.

AI leverage & promote-from-within4 + 4

AI chiefs-of-staff and reduced meeting time (4 voices); promote-from-within and the high hiring bar (4 voices).

Signals & Good Ideas

Not trends — one to three voices each, worth seeing anyway

Quote quality · 1 voice, hard claim
"Team is losing trust in our quotes"

A BM estimates ~80% of misquote discounts at his branch trace to CET-produced quotes. One voice — but it lands next to the measured 46%-within-20% estimate-accuracy number, so it warrants a data check, not a shrug.

Org idea · 1 voice
Regional sales QA role

Detailed proposal from a BM: a regional sales-quality position to train CET reps, audit quotes/ABs, streamline new-manager sales training, and absorb the hardest leads. Overlaps the sales-bandwidth conversation already in motion.

Goals design · 1–2 voices
Branch-scorecard-aligned quarterly goals

Set each branch's quarterly goals from its own scorecard gaps (e.g., a branch whose biggest gaps are NPS + mover eNPS shouldn't carry the same priorities as everyone else). Thoughtful, concrete, QGR-relevant.

Benefits paper-cut · 2 voices
The shrinking holiday count

Stated 8 holidays are effectively ≤7 for M–F staff in years when holidays land on weekends. Two independent voices; cheap to fix, outsized goodwill.

Measurement culture · 1 voice
The Goodhart warning

"The team can become better at delivering on a metric without actually moving the needle associated with it." A field manager articulating metric distortion unprompted — worth keeping in the room whenever a new KPI ships.

Knowledge base · 3 voices (emerging)
One brain for resources

Docs live across Slack, Monday, Drive, and a growing pile of apps: "Can we have one app that is the center and brain for resources?" Three independent voices — watch whether the mover survey echoes it.

Policy Change

This was the last mandatory survey

Starting next cycle, engagement surveys go voluntary. Two reasons. First, honesty: at 100% forced participation we probably have more than one true detractor — requiring a response can mask the signal it's supposed to surface. Second, participation itself becomes data: a branch that chooses 100% participation and a branch at 30% are telling us two very different things, and we'll read that per-branch rate as its own engagement signal going forward.

What that costs us: this cycle is the last clean apples-to-apples comparison against prior mandatory cycles. The first voluntary run sets a new baseline, and we'll grade trends from there. Anne owns the comms and the transition.
